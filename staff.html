Helper Application

Application Writing Guide

There’s no shame in asking for help, which is why we’re asking for your help, in particular, we’re asking you to apply for the Helper position by submitting an application. The following will tell you all you need to know about what a Helper does and how to apply for a Helper position. In essence, a Helper is an intern that works for us remotely and may be promoted to a paid position, though we can’t make any promises. You won’t be getting paid for the Helper position but you will get some cool perks that come with the position. The following tips will tell you how to write a good Helper application but the same tips apply to writing any job application or a CV, so if we don’t end up accepting your application, you’ll be getting some cool knowledge on how to apply for a job or write a CV.

Have a friendly tone

We’re not robots and don’t expect you to write like a robot either (unless you are a robot, in which case beep-beep-boop-boop). Feel free to show some personality, originality and humour in your Helper application. We’re looking for a person with distinct tastes and motivations, so do show us what kind of personality you’ve got.

Show motivation

Working in any kind of position requires that you are motivated and can stay motivated without anyone having to coax you into doing what you promised to do. In short, you should have a burning passion for doing the job and tell us straight away if you do or don’t have it.

Showcase your skills

The Helper position requires certain skills, such as being fair and objective without emotions getting in your way. If you’ve had experiences where you did just that, let us know about them. Don’t be modest or timid but also don’t boast or exaggerate. Simply tell us how you were fair and objective in a random event that caught you unprepared.

Have a friendly tone

Paragraphs are your friends. Break up walls of text into paragraphs and use subheadings, just like we did in this application writing guide. Place periods and commas where needed and don’t bold, italicise, underline or capitalise random letters and words. Don’t use fancy fonts or colours; Arial, Calibre or Times New Roman in black colour work just fine. Avoid fancy graphics as well since this is a simple application and we can’t expect you to spend hours of your life drawing charts and graphs. Spellcheck everything and try to avoid overloading the text with adjectives.

Show Make your text flow naturally

Paragraphs in your Helper application should have some kind of connection between them that makes them read and flow naturally. If you mention a skill or an event, don’t just leave them unexplained but do go into detail about what you meant with those references. We shouldn’t be left wandering, “What did the author mean to say with that?”

Back up your claims

Whenever you claim something, try to provide some sort of data to back your claims. Yeah, we understand you can’t provide us with peer-reviewed studies but we’re not asking for them, but simply that you give us some kind of an example, even if it’s an anecdote. Let’s say you tell us in your application that you’ve got a sense of duty, so tell us an anecdote where you acted that way.

Be creative 

We mentioned that you should be serious and presentable but that doesn’t mean you have to be bland and boring. If you can write your application as a cool poem, go for it. You’ll need creativity if you’re going to be working as a Helper, so try to squeeze in some creativity in your application too.

Tell us your story

We all have our stories that for the most part go untold. Try to tell us your life story and how your life shaped you to become the way you are. In most cases, it’s the hard parts that make us become better, so don’t be ashamed of telling us how you overcame some adversity or challenge. Trust us, you’ll encounter a lot of adversity and challenge working as a Helper, so the positive spirit and a sense of can-do will really go a long way.

Catch our attention straight away 

You can count on there being hundreds, if not thousands of applications and they’ll all be reading pretty much the same, with the writer meandering and stumbling throughout the application. We can already tell what the writer is going to say as soon as we see the first paragraph, so catch our attention straight away. Your first paragraph should be the best one and really hook us in. If you can do that, you’ve guaranteed that someone will read your application all the way through.

Don’t be ashamed of failing

Despite all your best efforts, your Helper application might still not be accepted. That’s OK and it’s not the end of the world. Don’t blame yourself or feel any shame, it just wasn’t your lucky day. This also means that you don’t have to write glowing praise for yourself in your application; it’s actually much more sincere to write about failures than successes.

Add a nice conclusion 

Your application shouldn’t just fizzle out but should end with a BANG, just like fireworks. You know how you can hear the sizzle and see the streak across the sky and you know to expect a BANG but it still makes you go, “Wow!” Try to add a conclusion that feels like that and don’t be afraid of surprising us.

Helper Application Format

PLEASE NOTE: The Helper application should not contain any PII (personally identifiable information), such as your e-mail address or full name. If we need to know more about you, we’ll let you know and send you specific questions. This is also good security advice to have in mind when choosing passwords, e-mail addresses etc. – minimise sharing your personal information with anyone online. The following is a walk-through on how to write and format the helper application. The walk-through is divided in subheadings, with each subheading providing more details on how to write and format it.

Basic information

Your application should contain basic information about you as a gamer and a gaming personality. Please tell us:

  • Your Minecraft username
  • Your Previous Minecraft usernames
  • Age: (must be 16+)
  • Timezone
  • Whether have access to a working microphone and Teamspeak
  • Whether you can record Minecraft video and if so, what resolution/frame-rate
  • The link to your Twitter profile dedicated to gaming, if applicable
  • Fluency/proficiency in languages other than English, if applicable

Vanilla SMP activity

We’d like to know what game types you play the most and whether you enjoy being in the game or on the forums more.

Time available

Mention how many hours a day/week/month you can dedicate to the Helper role.

Prior bans on the Vanilla SMP or in Minecraft

If you’ve been banned on the Vanilla SMP or in Minecraft, please let us know when it happened, why and how long it lasted.

Previous moderation experience 

Tell us in your application if you’ve got managerial experience, meaning that you organized a group of people (2-15) to do something. Even if it’s your Dungeons&Dragons clique, we’d like to know how you handle groups.

Previous applications

If you’ve made more than one Helper application, let us know how many there were and when the last one was submitted.
